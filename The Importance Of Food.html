The importance of food

Food is a source of life for living things, they cannot survive long if they stop eating. It is necessary to carry out the vital processes carried out by the body such as: growth, movement, building and regeneration of cells. Food protects the body from diseases, and provides it with immune bodies that protect it from the attack of viruses and harmful microbes. Food provides the body with protein responsible for building tissues and muscles, and helps in cell growth and permanent renewal. Carbohydrates help provide the person with the energy he needs through the group of carbohydrates and sugars that fall under their list. Fats and oils: help the body provide energy stores, and are used as a thermal insulator for the body. Dietary fiber: helps facilitate the movement of food through the digestive system, and prevents constipation. Vitamins are used in many bodily functions such as: red blood cell production, breathing, nervous control and other essential functions of the body. Mineral salts: They are elements that support the functions of organs and help them to complete their function in an ideal manner, and their deficiency in the body causes health and psychological problems.

    • healthy food for kids
    Children need healthy nutrition that contains all kinds of nutrients, such as: carbohydrates, protein, fats, vitamins, and minerals. However, the amounts needed by children vary according to age groups. Among the most important principles of healthy nutrition for children, we mention the following:
  • Choose seafood, lean meats, eggs, beans, peas, soy products, and unsalted seeds when eating protein.
  • Eat a variety of fresh, dried, canned or frozen fruits instead of fruit juice.
  • Eat a variety of fresh, canned, frozen and dried vegetables.
  • Choose whole grains, such as: whole wheat bread, popcorn, oats, quinoa, and wild or brown rice.
  • Limit the intake of refined grains, such as: white bread, pasta, and rice.
  • Choose fat-free or low-fat dairy products, such as: milk, yogurt, cheese, and fortified soy drinks.
  • Limit your intake of added sugars.
  • Limit your intake of saturated fats that come from foods of animal sources, such as red meat, poultry, and full-fat dairy products.

A diet rich in calcium also keeps teeth and bones strong and can reduce the risk of age-related osteoporosis.
